Frequently asked questions

What is Performance Max?

As the next generation of Smart Shopping and Local campaigns, Performance Max is a new goal-based campaign type that helps you to access all of Google Ads inventory from a single campaign. It's designed to complement your other campaigns to help you find more converting customers across all of Google's channels like YouTube, Display, Search, Discover, Gmail, and Maps.

Powered by your Google Merchant Center product feed and other inputs you provide, like conversion goals, budget, audience signals and creative assets, Performance Max will show the most relevant ads across Google channels.

In Europe, Performance Max campaigns can be used with any Comparison Shopping Service (CSS) you work with. The ads will show on general search results pages and on any other surfaces the CSS has opted in to.

Can Performance Max be used to target multiple goals?

Yes, you can optimize to one or more goals, such as online and in-store conversions, from a single Performance Max campaign. As a best practice, track the value for all your conversion goals. By doing this, Performance Max will maximize conversion value for your budget. If your goals share the same budget and geography, you can target multiple goals from a single Performance Max campaign. If you have a separate budget that cannot be combined with other goals, you can create separate Performance Max campaigns that optimize for different goals.

Are assets required if I link to a Google Merchant Center product feed?

Additional creative assets are not required if you create a Performance Max campaign with online sales goals and a Google Merchant Center product feed. However, the more high-quality creative assets you provide for text, images and video, the more ad formats Performance Max can create and the more inventory your ad can appear on. We may help auto-generate the following creative assets on your behalf if you do not add your own:

  • Videos are auto-generated based on the provided text and image assets.
  • Headlines and descriptions are auto-generated based on the provided final URL.
